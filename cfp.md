## Overview

We invite submissions to **AIxAccessibility 2026**, a workshop exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence and accessibility. We welcome full research papers, short papers, position papers, and demos that contribute original insights or tools at this intersection.

Accepted papers will be presented at the workshop and included in the workshop proceedings.

---

## Submission Topics

We encourage submissions on (but not limited to) the following topics:

**AI for Assistive Technology**
- Speech recognition and synthesis for communication aids
- Computer vision for navigation and scene understanding
- Natural language processing for augmentative and alternative communication (AAC)
- AI-powered captioning, transcription, and sign language recognition

**Accessible AI Systems**
- Universal design and accessibility guidelines for AI products
- Accessible conversational agents and chatbots
- Interface adaptations for users with visual, motor, or cognitive impairments
- Explainable AI for accessibility applications

**Fairness, Bias, and Representation**
- Bias in AI systems affecting people with disabilities
- Representation of disability in training datasets and benchmarks
- Ethical considerations in deploying AI for vulnerable populations
- Disability justice perspectives in AI research

**Participatory and Community-Centered Design**
- Co-design methods involving disabled communities
- Disability-led research and lived-experience perspectives
- Community-based evaluation methodologies

**Evaluation and Standards**
- New benchmarks and evaluation metrics for accessible AI
- Compliance and alignment with accessibility standards (WCAG, ADA, etc.)
- User studies and accessibility audits

## Submission Types

| Type | Length | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Full Paper** | 8 pages | Original research with complete experiments and results |
| **Short Paper** | 4 pages | Preliminary work, position statements, or focused contributions |
| **Extended Abstract** | 2 pages | Work-in-progress or demo descriptions |

All page limits are exclusive of references. Submissions may include an unlimited number of pages for references.

---

## Submission Instructions

- Submissions must be written in English and formatted using the **[ACL anthology style](https://acl-org.github.io/ACLPUB/formatting.html)**.
- All submissions must be **anonymized** for double-blind review.
- Submissions must be uploaded via the workshop submission portal (link forthcoming).
- Previously published work or work currently under review elsewhere is **not eligible**.

## Review Process

All submissions will undergo double-blind peer review by at least two members of the program committee. Reviews will evaluate submissions based on:

- **Relevance** to the workshop themes
- **Technical quality** and rigor
- **Novelty** and contribution to the field
- **Clarity** of writing and presentation

Authors of accepted papers will have the opportunity to revise their papers before the camera-ready deadline.
